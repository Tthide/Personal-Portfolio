About Me

Hello! I’m Thibault Borde, a UX Engineer with a double degree from EMSE (École des Mines de Saint-Étienne) and TU Wien, specializing in Human-Centered Computing and Media. My academic and professional journey has been shaped by a deep curiosity for technology, human behavior, and inclusive design. I thrive working with user experience, software development, and creative problem-solving, and I’m passionate about crafting digital experiences that are not only functional but also intuitive, accessible, and meaningful.

Why UX?

Ever since I was a kid, I always knew I wanted to create and design things, especially if it could help people in their daily life. I was (and still am) very curious about science and how things work. I would draw mechanisms that popped in my head, or build pipelines out of paper sheets to create a circuit for marbles.

Discovering UX principles on a random Instagram post was a game-changer for me. Indeed, I have always thought of designing and creating things for the sake of efficiency (a very engineer mindset indeed). However, I never really considered how people could be impacted by designs and that, ultimately, it is people that use and should benefit from the things we create. That’s when it clicked:

Engineering shouldn’t just be about making systems work; it should also be about making them enjoyable, intuitive, and meaningful for the people who use them.
